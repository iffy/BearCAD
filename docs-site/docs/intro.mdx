BearCAD is a free, parametric 3D CAD app for designing real parts on your own computer —
nothing is uploaded anywhere. You sketch 2D shapes, give them exact dimensions, and pull them
into solid bodies you can export for 3D printing or other CAD tools (STL and STEP).

**Parametric** means your design stays editable: dimensions can be named values like `leg` or
`thickness`, and changing a value later reshapes the whole part to match.

![The BearCAD window: the Elements pane, 3D viewport, and Context/Parameters panes](/img/screenshots/elements-pane.png)

The window has four main areas:

- **The 3D viewport** (center) — where you draw, build, and look at your part.
- **The Elements pane** (left) — every sketch, feature, and body in your design, as a list,
  tree, or graph.
- **The Context pane** (right) — options for whatever tool or selection is active.
- **The Parameters pane** (right) — your named values, editable any time.

## Getting BearCAD

- **[Run it in your browser](https://www.iffycan.com/BearCAD/app/)** — nothing to install.
  The web version can't cut holes into solids yet (that needs the desktop geometry engine),
  and documents save as downloads rather than to browser storage.
- **[Download it](https://github.com/iffy/BearCAD/releases/latest)** for macOS, Windows, or
  Linux — the full experience, including scripting.
